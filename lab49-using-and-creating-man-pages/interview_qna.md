Q1. What are man pages in Linux?
A: Built-in documentation for commands and utilities.

Q2. How do you view a man page?
A: Using man <command>.

Q3. What does the number in ls(1) represent?
A: The manual section number.

Q4. What are common man page sections?
A: NAME, SYNOPSIS, DESCRIPTION, OPTIONS, AUTHOR.

Q5. What tool is used to write man pages?
A: roff/groff formatting.

Q6. Why are man pages compressed?
A: To save disk space.

Q7. Where are custom man pages stored?
A: /usr/local/share/man/.

Q8. What does mandb do?
A: Updates the man page database.

Q9. Why create custom man pages?
A: To document scripts professionally.

Q10. Why are man pages important for administrators?
A: They provide reliable offline documentation.
