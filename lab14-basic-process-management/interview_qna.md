Q1. What is a process in Linux?  
A: A running instance of a program.

Q2. What does ps aux display?  
A: A snapshot of all running processes with detailed information.

Q3. What does PID stand for?  
A: Process ID.

Q4. How is top different from ps?  
A: top shows real-time process activity; ps shows a snapshot.

Q5. Why is top useful for administrators?  
A: It helps identify resource-heavy processes in real time.

Q6. How do you terminate a process by PID?  
A: Using the kill command.

Q7. What signal does kill send by default?  
A: SIGTERM.

Q8. When should kill -9 be used?  
A: When a process does not terminate normally.

Q9. What does pkill do?  
A: Terminates processes by name.

Q10. Why is pkill -x safer?  
A: It matches the exact process name.
