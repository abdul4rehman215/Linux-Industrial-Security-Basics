Q1. What are Linux system logs?
A: Files that record system, kernel, and application events.

Q2. Where are system logs stored?
A: Mostly under the /var/log directory.

Q3. What does syslog contain?
A: General system and service messages.

Q4. What is auth.log used for?
A: Authentication and security-related events.

Q5. What is kern.log?
A: Kernel-related system messages.

Q6. How do you safely view large log files?
A: Using the less command.

Q7. Why use grep with logs?
A: To quickly filter specific events or errors.

Q8. What does tail -f do?
A: Displays log entries in real time.

Q9. How do you stop tail -f?
A: Press Ctrl + C.

Q10. Why are logs important in cybersecurity?
A: They help detect attacks, failures, and suspicious activity.
