Q1. What does the du command do?  
A: It calculates disk usage of files and directories.

Q2. What does du -sh show?  
A: Summary disk usage in human-readable format.

Q3. Why use the -h flag?  
A: To display sizes in MB or GB.

Q4. What does du -sh Documents/* show?  
A: Sizes of subdirectories inside Documents.

Q5. How does sort -hr help?  
A: It sorts disk usage from largest to smallest.

Q6. Why identify large directories?  
A: To prevent disk space exhaustion.

Q7. What does du -ah include?  
A: Both files and directories.

Q8. Why use head with du output?  
A: To limit results to the largest items.

Q9. Where is disk usage analysis commonly used?  
A: Servers and storage monitoring.

Q10. Why is disk monitoring important?  
A: To maintain system stability and performance.
